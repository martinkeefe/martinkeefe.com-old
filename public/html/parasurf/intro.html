Introduction

A parametric surface is a 3-dimensional surface defined by a set of parametric equations with two parameters. These equations map points \((u,v)\) in some 2-dimensional space \(D\) (the domain) to the position vectors of the points on the 3-dimensional surface \(S\). This parametric representation can be written:

$$\vec{r}(u,v) = x(u,v)\,\vec{i} + y(u,v)\,\vec{j} + z(u,v)\,\vec{k}$$

The vectors \(\vec{i}\), \(\vec{j}\) and \(\vec{k}\) are the basis vectors of the 3-dimensional space that the surface is embedded in. For Cartesian coordinates in the normal Euclidean space \(\mathbb{R}^3\) these are the familiar X, Y and Z axes.

For example, the parametric representation of a cone is:

$$\vec{r}(u,v) = u\cos v\,\vec{i} + u\sin v\,\vec{j} + u\,\vec{k}$$

So the parametric equations are:

$$\begin{align} x& = u\cos v\\ y& = u\sin v\\ z& = u \end{align}$$

And the domain is:

$$\begin{align} u& \in \mathbb{R}\\ v& \in \mathbb{R}, \; -\pi \leq v \lt \pi \end{align}$$
