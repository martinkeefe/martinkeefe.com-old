7. \text and \mod

7.1 The \text command

The main use of the command \text is for words or phrases in a display.

f_{[x_{i-1},x_i]} \text{ is monotonic,}
\quad i = 1,\dots,c+1
 \[f_{[x_{i-1},x_i]} \text{ is monotonic,} \quad i = 1,\dots,c+1\]

7.2. \mod and its relatives

Commands \mod, \bmod, \pmod, \pod deal with the special spacing conventions of “mod” notation. \mod and \pod are variants of \pmod preferred by some authors; \mod omits the parentheses, whereas \pod omits the “mod” and retains the parentheses.

\begin{gather}
\gcd(n,m\bmod n)\\
\quad x\equiv y\pmod b\\
\quad x\equiv y\mod c\\
\quad x\equiv y\pod d
\end{gather}
 \[ \begin{gather} \gcd(n,m\bmod n)\\ \quad x\equiv y\pmod b\\ \quad x\equiv y\mod c\\ \quad x\equiv y\pod d \end{gather} \]
